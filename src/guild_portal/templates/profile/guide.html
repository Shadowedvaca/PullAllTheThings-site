Member Features
🚀 Getting Started ▶

What is this website?

The {{ site().guild_name }} website is the guild's hub — it tracks your characters, lets you vote on campaigns, shows the raid schedule, lets you browse what guildies can craft, and monitors market prices for raid consumables.

How do I get an account?

Accounts are created via invite code. DM the guild bot on Discord and ask for an invite — it will send you a registration link automatically. You can also ask an officer to generate one from the admin panel.

What can my account do?

  • Set up your profile with your main character and spec
  • Link your Battle.net account to verify and auto-claim your WoW characters
  • Mark your weekly availability for raid planning
  • Vote in guild campaigns (art votes, polls, book club picks, etc.)
  • Request guild crafting orders via the Crafting Corner
  • Opt in to crafting notifications when someone requests something you can make
💡 Your rank in the Settings sidebar matches your in-game guild rank and Discord role — it's kept in sync automatically.
👤 Your Profile ▶

Settings → My Profile

This is your personal settings page. Here you can:

  • Display Name — What your name shows as across the site. Defaults to your Discord server name.
  • Main Character — Your primary raiding character. Shows up on the public roster and is used for Wowhead comp links.
  • Main Spec — Your preferred spec for the main character.
  • Secondary Character / Spec — Optional offspec or alt you raid on.
  • Timezone — Used to display event times in your local time.
  • Auto-invite to events — When checked, you'll automatically be added as Accepted on Raid-Helper events. Uncheck if you prefer to sign up manually.
  • Crafting notifications — When checked, the bot will @mention you in #crafters-corner when someone requests something you can craft.

Claiming Characters

If your character doesn't appear linked yet, you can claim an unclaimed guild character from the dropdown at the bottom of the profile page. Linking your Battle.net account (see below) is the easiest way — it auto-claims all your characters on the guild's home realm.

💡 Your main character's item level and spec are pulled automatically from the Blizzard API and stay current after each sync.
🔵 Battle.net Account ▶

What it does

Linking your Battle.net account lets the site verify which WoW characters are actually yours and auto-claim them — no manual claiming or officer intervention needed. Characters claimed via Battle.net show a lock badge and cannot be accidentally unlinked or reassigned.

How to link

On your Profile page (Settings → My Profile), click "Connect Battle.net". You'll be redirected to Blizzard's login page. After authorizing, you're sent back to the site and your characters are synced automatically.

What gets synced

  • All your WoW characters on the guild's home realm at level 10 or above
  • Characters are upserted — new ones are added, existing ones are updated
  • Claims are marked as Battle.net Verified (high-confidence) on your profile and in the Player Manager

Unlinking

You can disconnect your Battle.net account from the profile page. This removes the OAuth token but leaves your claimed characters in place — they just become manually-claimable again.

Automatic refresh

Battle.net tokens are refreshed nightly at 3:15 AM UTC. If your token expires, re-linking from the profile page will restore sync.

💡 The onboarding flow automatically prompts new members to link their Battle.net account as part of joining the guild website.
🗳️ Voting & Campaigns ▶

How voting works

When a campaign is active, you'll see a voting card on the home page. Click it to go to the voting page. Campaigns use ranked-choice voting — you rank your top picks in order of preference (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.).

Submitting your vote

Drag entries into your ranking order, or use the rank selectors. You can change your vote any time while the campaign is still open. Once you submit, your current rankings are saved — submitting again overwrites the previous vote.

Results

Results are shown after a campaign closes. Ranked-choice works by eliminating the lowest-ranked entry each round and redistributing votes until a winner emerges.

Campaign types

  • Image vote — Pick from artwork submissions (e.g. guild banner art)
  • Poll — Vote on options like raid nights, event ideas, etc.
💡 The Discord bot posts announcements to the campaign channel at voting milestones — keep an eye out for those.
📅 Your Availability ▶

Settings → My Profile (Availability section)

The availability grid is at the bottom of your Profile page. For each day of the week, you can mark:

  • Whether you're generally available that day
  • Your earliest start time (in your timezone)
  • How many hours you can play

What it's used for

Officers see a 7-day availability grid in the admin panel that aggregates all members' availability. This helps with scheduling raid nights — the grid shows role distribution and weighted scores per day so officers can pick optimal nights.

💡 This is general weekly availability, not a per-event signup. Set it once and update it when your schedule changes.
⚒️ Crafting Corner ▶

Browsing recipes

Go to /crafting-corner (linked in the top nav as "Crafting"). Browse by profession and expansion tier. Each recipe shows which guild members can craft it.

Placing a guild order

Find the recipe you need and click "Request Guild Order". The bot will immediately post an embed in #crafters-corner on Discord, tagging all guild members who know that recipe. They can respond directly in Discord to arrange the craft.

Crafting notifications

If you're a crafter, go to your Profile (Settings → My Profile) and enable Crafting notifications. When that's on, the bot will @mention you specifically when someone requests something you can make.

Recipe data

The recipe directory is synced automatically from the Blizzard API. It stays current as guild members learn new recipes.

📈 Market Watch ▶

What it is

The Market Watch card on the home page shows current Auction House prices for raid-relevant consumables — flasks, injectors, enchants, and gems. Prices are updated hourly from the Blizzard AH API.

What's tracked

  • Consumables — Flasks and health injectors used in progression raiding
  • Enchants — Weapon and cloak enchants relevant to current content
  • Gems — Jeweler's settings for gear sockets

Price display

Prices shown are the current minimum buyout (or region-wide market price for commodities). Each item links to Wowhead for full details. Officers can configure which items are tracked and trigger manual syncs from the AH Pricing admin page.

💡 Commodity items (flasks, gems) use region-wide pricing from the Blizzard commodity endpoint. Non-commodity items use your guild's connected realm.
🤖 The Discord Bot ▶

What the bot does automatically

  • Role sync — Your Discord rank stays in sync with your in-game guild rank. When your rank changes, the bot updates your Discord roles automatically.
  • Crafting orders — Posts embeds in #crafters-corner when a guild order is placed on the website, tagging relevant crafters.
  • Campaign announcements — Posts milestone updates in the campaign channel when voting is active.
  • Raid event booking — After a raid event starts, the bot auto-creates next week's Raid-Helper signup sheet so it's ready before you log off for the night.
  • Attendance tracking — When enabled, the bot monitors the raid voice channel and logs join/leave events to calculate attendance for each raid event.

Onboarding new members

When a new member joins the Discord, the bot automatically starts an onboarding conversation via DM. It walks you through getting a website account and linking your Battle.net account. If you miss a step, the bot follows up after 24 hours and again at 48 hours before closing the flow.

Getting an invite

DM the guild bot directly and ask for a website invite — it will send you a registration code and link automatically. You can also ask an officer and they'll generate one from the admin panel.

💡 Don't see the guild bot in your DMs? Make sure your Discord privacy settings allow DMs from server members, then try again.

⚔️ Raid Tools ▶

Settings → Raid Tools

The Raid Tools page is for creating and managing Raid-Helper events directly from the website, without needing to paste commands into Discord.

Availability Grid

At the top of the page is a 7-day availability grid. Each card shows:

  • How many members are available that day and what percentage of the active roster that is
  • A weighted score (Officers and Veterans count more than Initiates)
  • Role breakdown (Tank / Healer / Melee / Ranged counts)
  • If a recurring event is scheduled that day, it shows at the top of the card

Click any day card to expand it and see the list of available players with their role and rank.

Event Builder

Clicking a day card pre-fills the event builder form with that day's recurring event details. You can then adjust the title, type, date, time, duration, and description before creating the event.

Roster Preview

Below the form, the Roster Preview shows all active players with their auto-invite status (Accepted / Tentative / Bench). You can override individual players' status before creating the event.

Creating the event

Click "Create Event" to submit to Raid-Helper and create the signup sheet in Discord. The event is also saved in the database for attendance tracking.

Manual Fallback

If Raid-Helper is unavailable, there's a collapsible "Manual Fallback" section at the bottom that generates a Discord-formatted event description you can paste manually.

🛡️ Player Manager ▶

Settings → Player Manager

The Player Manager is the primary tool for maintaining the guild roster identity. It has three columns:

  • Discord — Live Discord server members, pulled directly from the bot
  • Players — Guild identity records (the player entities that own characters and have ranks)
  • Characters — WoW characters from the guild roster (synced via Blizzard API and the GuildSync addon)

Linking Discord to Players

Drag a Discord member from the left column onto a Player card in the middle column to link their Discord identity. This enables role sync and bot DMs for that player.

Linking Characters to Players

Drag a Character from the right column onto a Player card to assign ownership. You can also toggle whether a character is the player's Main or an Alt.

Battle.net Verified Characters

Characters claimed via Battle.net OAuth show a 🔒 badge and are locked — they cannot be dragged or manually reassigned. This protects verified identity data. To reassign a locked character, the player must disconnect their Battle.net account first.

Attendance Dot

Each player card shows a colored attendance dot (green / amber / red / grey) based on their attendance rate over the configured trailing window. Grey means new member or feature disabled.

Drill-down

Click the ◎ button on any card to filter all three columns to only show items related to that player. A gold banner shows the active filter — click ✕ to clear it.

Aliases

Player cards can have alias chips — alternate names (nicknames, old character names) that help the identity engine match guild notes to the right player. Add aliases using the + alias button on a player card.

Characters with ? badge

A ? badge on a character means it wasn't found in the Blizzard API scan. This usually means a realm slug mismatch. You can delete these placeholder characters with the ✕ button.

📊 Data Quality ▶

Settings → Data Quality

The Data Quality page monitors the health of the guild identity data — character links, Discord connections, and role assignments.

Coverage Dashboard

The top section shows overall matching coverage — what percentage of guild characters are linked to players, broken down by confidence level (High / Medium / Low) and link source (note key, name match, manual, Battle.net OAuth, etc.).

Audit Rules

Seven automated rules run after each guild sync:

  • orphan_wow — A WoW character with no linked player
  • orphan_discord — A Discord member with no linked player
  • role_mismatch — Player's recorded rank doesn't match their Discord role
  • stale_character — Character hasn't been seen in a long time
  • duplicate_discord — Two players linked to the same Discord user
  • stale_discord_link — Player linked to a Discord user who left the server
  • main_char_not_linked — A player's main/offspec pointer is orphaned

Drift Detection

The Drift Detection panel at the bottom tracks things that were correct and have since changed. Run a drift scan manually with the "Run Drift Scan" button, or it runs automatically after each sync.

7-Day Grid

🎙️ Raid Attendance ▶

Settings → Attendance

Tracks raid attendance by combining two sources: voice channel presence (bot logs who is in the raid voice channel) and Warcraft Logs reports (who appears in WCL for that session). The result is a season-wide grid showing attendance for every player across every event.

Season Grid

The main view is a player × event matrix. Each cell shows:

  • ✓ green — Attended. Flags: ⏰ arrived late, 🚪 left early.
  • ✗ red — Absent (processed, not present)
  • ~ amber — Excused absence
  • [live] blue — Event in progress, tracking now
  • — grey — No data for this event

The rightmost column shows each player's total attendance count and percentage. The bottom row shows guild-wide attendance per event.

Habitual Patterns

Players who are consistently late or leave early across recent raids get a ⚠ Habitual badge. Hover it to see the pattern. The bot also posts an alert to the audit channel when a habitual pattern is detected.

Cell Popover

Click any cell for details: attendance source, minutes present, join/leave times, late/early flags. You can mark a cell as Excused directly from the popover.

Event Detail Panel

Click a column header to open the event detail panel — shows the full per-player breakdown for that event including time in channel, data source, and result. A Re-process button re-runs both the WCL and voice passes for that event.

Unlinked Users

Discord users seen in voice who aren't linked to a player appear in the collapsible "Unlinked Discord Users" list at the bottom of the grid. Use the Player Manager to link them.

Settings

  • Enable voice attendance tracking — Master toggle. The bot must have View Channel + Connect permission on the raid voice channel.
  • Minimum presence % — How much of the event a player must be present to count as attended (default 75%).
  • Late arrival grace — Minutes after event start before arriving counts as "late" (default 10 min).
  • Early leave grace — Minutes before event end that leaving counts as "left early" (default 10 min).
  • Trailing window — How many recent events the attendance dot on Player Manager uses (default 8).
  • Habitual flag window / threshold — How many events to look back and how many occurrences to flag as habitual (default: 5 events, 3 occurrences).

CSV Export

The CSV Export button downloads a full season attendance spreadsheet with one row per player and one column per event.

💡 Attendance is processed automatically every 30 minutes for events that ended more than 30 minutes ago. You can also re-process any event manually from the event detail panel.
⚙️ Crafting Sync ▶

Settings → Crafting Sync

This page manages the automated profession data sync from the Blizzard API.

Sync Cadence

The sync cadence adapts automatically based on the season:

  • New expansion: Daily for 28 days after season start (recipes change frequently)
  • Established season: Daily for 14 days after season start, then weekly
  • Ongoing: Weekly

Season Configuration

Set the current expansion name, season number, and season start date. This drives the adaptive cadence logic.

Force Refresh

Use the "Force Refresh" button to trigger an immediate full sync — useful right after a new patch drops and recipes change.

Sync Stats

The page shows the last sync time, duration, how many characters were processed, and how many recipes were found.

📦 AH Pricing ▶

Settings → AH Pricing

Manages which items are tracked for Auction House pricing and lets you manually trigger a sync. Prices from this page power the Market Watch card on the public home page.

Tracked Items

The item list shows all currently tracked consumables, enchants, and gems. Each row shows the item name, category, current price, and last-updated time. You can add new items, toggle existing items on/off, and reorder them.

Realm Setup

The Resolve Realm button looks up your guild's connected realm ID from the Blizzard API using the realm slug from Site Config. This ID is cached in the database and used for realm-specific auction lookups.

Manual Sync

Click Sync Now to trigger an immediate price update outside the hourly schedule. Useful after a major patch or when you've just added new items.

Price Source

Commodity items (flasks, gems — things anyone can buy from the AH without a specific seller) use the Blizzard region-wide commodities endpoint. Non-commodity items (specific enchant scrolls) fall back to your connected realm's auction data.

💡 Prices sync hourly at :15 past each hour. The home page card only appears if at least one active item has a price on record.
📣 Campaigns ▶

Settings → Social Tools → Campaigns

Create and manage guild voting campaigns from this page.

Campaign lifecycle

  • Draft — Not visible to members yet. Set it up and add entries.
  • Live — Members can vote. Shows on the home page as an active campaign.
  • Closed — Voting has ended. Results are calculated and displayed.

Creating a campaign

Set the title, description, type (ranked choice / poll), how many picks voters get, minimum rank to vote, start time, and duration. You can also link a Discord channel for bot announcements.

Agent chattiness

The contest agent posts milestone updates to Discord (e.g. "halfway there!", "one hour left!"). Set chattiness to:

  • Normal — Milestone posts at standard intervals
  • Quiet — Fewer posts
  • Off — No Discord posts

Early close

If "Close early if all eligible members have voted" is checked, the campaign closes automatically once everyone with voting access has submitted.

📜 Warcraft Logs ▶

Settings → Warcraft Logs

Integrates with the Warcraft Logs API to pull parse data and raid reports. Used as a data source for attendance alongside voice tracking.

Configuration

Enter your WCL API client ID and secret (from your Warcraft Logs account), then verify the connection. Once connected, the sync runs automatically after each raid sync pass.

Reports

The Reports tab shows recent WCL reports for your guild — log date, zone, duration, and a direct link to the full report on Warcraft Logs. Reports are used to reconcile attendance: anyone who appears in a WCL report for a raid event is credited as attended.

Parses

The Parses tab shows per-player performance data pulled from WCL — best parse percentile per boss, spec, and difficulty. This feeds the public /roster parse display.

Attendance (WCL source)

The WCL-sourced attendance pass runs for each processed raid event: it matches WCL report participants to guild players by character name and marks them as attended. When a player is in WCL but not in voice (or vice versa), the combined result is still counted as attended.

💡 WCL data requires a Warcraft Logs account and active logging during raids. If logs aren't uploaded, the voice channel pass is the sole attendance source for that event.
🏆 Progression ▶

Settings → Progression

Tracks raid progression and M+ activity across the guild, syncing data from the Blizzard API, Raider.IO, and Warcraft Logs.

Raid Progression

Per-character boss kill data is synced from the Blizzard encounters API. The public /roster Progression tab aggregates this into a guild-wide view showing kills per boss across difficulties.

Mythic+ Tracking

The current M+ season ID (set on this page) controls which season's keystone data is pulled. Per-character best key levels and scores sync automatically as part of the weekly progression pass.

Tracked Achievements

Add specific achievement IDs to track across the guild. On the next sync, the system checks each character for those achievements and stores completion status. Useful for tracking guild-wide progress on meta achievements or raid titles.

Snapshots

The weekly sweep (Sundays at 4:30 AM UTC) takes a full progression snapshot — a point-in-time record of every character's kill counts. Snapshots let you compare progression week over week.

Sync Stats

The page shows when the last progression sync ran, how many characters were processed, and the last snapshot date.

💡 Characters that haven't logged in recently are skipped to save API quota. Use the "Force Full" option on the sync trigger to include all characters regardless of last login.
