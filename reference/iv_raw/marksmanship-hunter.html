4.

Ascendant and Nebulous Voidcores (Bonus Rolls)

Patch 12.0.5 introduces two new gearing systems that you need to familiarize yourself with:

  • Nebulous Voidcore is a new currency that can be used to "Bonus Roll" on certain dungeons and items. Using it will guarantee a drop from that source, but the item you get is random. However, you are protected from repeats. Use this enough times on a boss or dungeon, and you are guaranteed your desired item.
  • Ascendant Voidcore extends the upgrade capabilities of Weapons and Trinkets, letting you upgrade them even further than before using your Crests.

For more information on how to acquire these items, make sure to check out our dedicated guide below.

Voidforge and Voidcores Guide
4.1.

Recommended Nebulous Voidcore Usage for Marksmanship Hunter

Obviously, the straightforward answer to this question is to use the Voidcores on the best upgrade that you have not yet gotten. This is unique to your character, but I will offer a list of some of the most important items for Marksmanship Hunter, with the goal being that you can just go down the list and chase the #1 thing you see that you do not already have.

It is also worth noting that Bonus Rolling for an item that you may be able to get from an upcoming raid boss drop could be a bad idea, depending on how you value "power now" versus "power later".

Voidcore (Bonus Roll) Priority
  1. Mythic Track Weapon
  2. Algeth'ar Puzzle Box
  3. Mythic Umbral Plume
  4. Remaining BiS List Items
4.2.

Ascendant Voidcores

Using your Ascendant Voidcores follows a similar priority list as using your Crests:

  1. Your Weapon is the highest priority.
  2. Your Algeth'ar Puzzle Box.
  3. Your other Trinket.

Generally, focus on upgrading Myth Track items, as the currency is limited. One exception may be for the Algeth'ar Puzzle Box, because it is quite difficult to obtain at Myth Track from Great Vault.

Best Crafted Items for Marksmanship Hunter

You can have two Embellishments, which are special effects that come from Crafted Gear. In other words, all good gearsets have two Embellished, crafted items, and below, I will explain the best Embellishments to use, and what to craft them on.

The big decision is whether to prioritize crafting a weapon or other pieces first. World Tender's is endgame BiS; but early on, the iLvl increase of a Weapon is tough to pass up. However, the first boss on Mythic offers a weapon, and your Mythic+ Great Vault offers another chance of getting a weapon.

  • Option 1: World Tender's Rootslippers/ World Tender's Barkclasp — Your BiS in the long run, and a "safe" Craft in the sense that they will never be replaced. However, if you Craft this and you do not get a Mythic Weapon drop for weeks on end, it can be worse than Crafting a Weapon.
  • Option 2: Aln'hara Sprigshot with Darkmoon Sigil: Hunt — In a way, this is "safer" as its minimum DPS is higher, but it does put you on an awkward path and you will not finalize your BiS Crafts for quite a long time. On the bright side, you have a guaranteed chunk of extra power early on, and you will not have a lot of anxiety about getting a Mythic weapon to drop.
  • Option 3: Wait as long as you possibly can to see if you get a Mythic Weapon drop, and then decide afterwards. The longer you can wait, the better. You could wait until after your kill of the first boss on Mythic, you could wait until your first Vault, and so on.
  • For myself, as a World #150 Hall of Fame guild player with another Hunter in my raid, I am likely going to craft the World Tender Set after seeing what the first boss drops. My co-Hunter is likely going to craft Weapon at the same time. That way, at least one of us will get full value out of a weapon if it drops, we are sort of hedging our bets.
Marksmanship Hunter Crafting Slot Priority Weapon or Set Bonus → Neck → Rings → Cloak/Wrist

You should ideally sim the optimal stats to craft for your particular character, but Crit/Mastery is most likely your best option with a Thalassian Missive of the Peerless.

You do not need to have a Professions to get gear crafted for you, that is what Crafting Order NPC's are for, but if you are interested in crafting for yourself and saving some Gold, check out our Professions Overview page:

Midnight Professions Overview
7.

Crest Usage

You will be able to upgrade your items via Crests such as Myth Dawncrest. Once you have upgraded a slot to a certain item level, upgrading other items of that slot to that item level is free.

When you upgrade with Crests, you should think of it as upgrading slots and not items.

Additionally, since Crests are a limited resource, you will want to obtain the highest item-level possible before upgrading a given slot, as this means you will spend fewer Crests on that slot in total. If you are at all capable of doing a +10 Dungeon, for example, it would be a VERY bad idea to upgrade an item from a +9 Dungeon, because you just wasted 20 Crests that could have been saved by just farming the slot on a +10.

We see a ton of players, even at a high level, mess up their upgrade priorities, and upgrading prematurely. Getting this right will put you far ahead of most players.

Below is the slot priority you should upgrade your items in. If you ever want to deviate from this, you should have a very good reason.

Marksmanship Hunter Crest Upgrade Priority Weapon → Trinkets → Helmet/Chest/Legs → Shoulder/Gloves/Belt/Boots → Rings/Neck → Cloak/Bracer

Note that exceptions are possible, such as special items that have more Item Level scaling than their slot would imply.

You can learn more about the Gear Upgrade System in our Gear Upgrade System page.
