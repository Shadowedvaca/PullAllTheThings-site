1.

Best in Slot Gear Guide for Brewmaster Monk

When first reaching Level 90 in Midnight, you will likely have plenty of low-level gear from questing equipped. Your initial item upgrades will largely come from further exploring the world and participating in Delves or Normal dungeons. Although the bulk of your ideal pieces of gear will come from selecting items within the Great Vault every week, raiding The Voidspire, The Dreamrift, or March on Quel'Danas on Mythic, completing very high keys in Mythic+, or even acquiring items crafted by Professions using the Crafting Orders system and Spark of Radiance, not all of this content will be feasible for everyone.

Fortunately, gearing as a Brewmaster is generally simple for most item slots: use the highest item-level gear you can get while trying to avoid unnecessary Haste. However, even an item that is 5 item levels higher with Haste will often still be an upgrade, so do not go entirely out of your way to avoid the stat. Rings are sometimes a different matter, as they do not contain Agility or Armor. However, unless you are trying to maximize your damage output, you can still safely equip a ring with a higher item level, no matter what its stats are. To learn about the rest of your stat priority, see our dedicated page for it.

2.3.

Midnight Season 1 Raid Gear BiS List for Brewmaster Monks

This table exclusively contains loot from The Voidspire, The Dreamrift, and March on Quel'Danas raids, alongside crafted embellishments. Although it is potentially easier to acquire, particularly pieces of your Tier Set, you will only have one chance to obtain gear per boss on each difficulty every week. In addition, there may be alternative items that are obtained elsewhere as part of your overall best-in-slot gear. However, these are the items from just the raid to look for.

TipsWhich Raid Drops Are Most Important To Brewmaster?
The following items are the ones you should seek out first; some of them may be highly contested by other specializations, particularly in offensive trinkets:
  • Inescapable Reach: This weapon drops from Vorasius, the second boss of The Voidspire, and is your BiS piece. Only a few specializations can equip it, allowing you to avoid crafting a weapon if a Mythic version of it drops early in progression.

  • Gaze of the Alnseer: Your second-strongest passive trinket for both damage and defense, dropped by Chimaerus in The Dreamrift; likely in demand by other specializations.
  • Ranger-Captain's Iridescent Insignia: If the two trinkets above are in high demand, this raw-damage alternative from the Crown of the Cosmos in The Voidspire is likely more available to you as a Brewmaster while remaining a potential BiS option at lower target counts.

FAQShould I Dual-Wield or Use a Two-Handed Weapon?

In Midnight's first season, your best choice for a weapon is to use a 2h over dual-wielding for a slight damage advantage. However, the best-in-slot dual-wield choices ( Dreadflail Bludgeon and Soulblight Cleaver) can offer similar performance, particularly in lower target counts. Equip whichever BiS setup you acquire first, defaulting to a two-handed option when you are choosing between gear of the same item level.

FAQWhich Tier Set Pieces Do I Want?

Season 1 of Midnight features a new set of Monk-themed gear to collect, known as the Way of Ra-Den's Chosen set. Pieces of this armor can be earned from bosses in The Voidspire/The Dreamrift/March on Quel'Danas raids, alongside any of the nine slots in the weekly Great Vault, and can also gradually be made at the Matrix Catalyst as a catchup system.

As there are five total tier slots, but only four are needed to gain their equipment bonuses, we recommend that you consider ignoring the leg piece in favor of the remaining four. However, getting your set bonuses takes priority over getting the "correct" pieces; all five remain very close in overall performance.

3.

Useful Trinkets for Brewmaster Monk

Trinkets are a gear slot that can provide shifting value across different areas of content. They are also the easiest piece to alter how offensive or defensive-minded you wish to be. Because of this, you should keep many of the trinkets you obtain and be ready to swap them out when the situation calls for it. If you are only interested in the absolute best general choices, then look to collect the damage/hybrid and defensive choices below.

Brewmaster Monk Best Trinkets Damage/Hybrid - Radiant Plume, Gaze of the Alnseer, or Ranger-Captain's Iridescent Insignia
Defense - Gloom-Spattered Dreadscale or Ampoule of Pure Void

Bloodmallet can also be used to see a comprehensive grouping of all available trinkets for Brewmaster Monks organized by their damage capabilities. If the source of the trinket is not listed on mouseover, then it only appears from world quests or events and will be difficult to acquire.

Finally, note that the items are listed in order of their approximate strength, bearing in mind the item level or upgrade tracks players can obtain them at. If a trinket is not discussed here, then it is not worth seeking out or holding onto over higher item level alternatives.

4.

Ascendant and Nebulous Voidcores (Bonus Rolls)

Patch 12.0.5 reintroduces some familiar systems to players, albeit in a new way: bonus rolls and additional upgrade levels. With the help of the new Voidforge, you may choose to obtain a guaranteed item from a given dungeon or boss loot pool at Hero or Myth track when completing the relevant keystone or encounter each week. You may also upgrade certain pieces of gear beyond their normal maximum item level for further power. This takes the form of two new currencies:

  • Nebulous Voidcore — Two of these can be obtained per week, allowing you to earn a random item from a given dungeon or boss loot pool by completing its keystone or encounter at the appropriate difficulty level. Each roll of a dungeon's loot table will require one Voidcore, while a raid encounter will require 2 and can include "very rare" drops. In addition, any items you have already received this way will be removed from future rolls, eventually guaranteeing the item you want!
  • Ascendant Voidcore — This currency is obtainable from endgame content sources and may be used to upgrade weapons and trinkets—including crafted ones—beyond their normal maximum. However, they must first be fully upgraded.

For more information on how to acquire these items, be sure to check out our dedicated guide below.

Voidforge and Voidcores Guide
4.1.

Recommended Nebulous Voidcore Usage for Brewmaster Monk

Due to the wide variety of loot a player may have been offered over time by the Great Vault in particular, it is difficult to provide simple recommendations for where to spend one's Nebulous Voidcores. However, due to these voidcores rolling against the entire loot table of a dungeon or raid encounter, some locations are a more effective use than others for your most convenient power gains.

Nebulous Voidcore (Bonus Roll) Priority
  1. Chimaerus ( Gaze of the Alnseer/ Alnscorned Spire)
  2. Seat of the Triumvirate (multiple BiS items and pieces for the Matrix Catalyst)
  3. Mythic Radiant Plume OR Mythic Gloom-Spattered Dreadscale
  4. Remaining BiS List items
4.2.

Ascendant Voidcore Priority

When it comes to Ascendant Voidcores, your options to use them on are much more limited, as they may only be applied to weapons and trinkets that are Hero or Myth track (including crafted ones). Naturally, this means you should aim to save them for your best-in-slot options on the lists above, which you can fortunately obtain eventually with the help of the Nebulous Voidcores earnable each week. Prioritize upgrading your items in the following order:

  1. Weapon (Myth 2h)
  2. Weapon (1h mainhand or crafted 2h)
  3. Trinket 1 ( Gaze of the Alnseer)
  4. Weapon (1h offhand or crafted)
  5. Trinket 2 ( Radiant Plume/ Gloom-Spattered Dreadscale)
5.

Recommended Crafted Gear and Embellishments

When gearing, you have the option to utilize profession gear to a greater extent than ever before, including the ability to create powerful items at an item level of up to 285. However, acquiring items of suitable strength is limited by a unique reagent known as a Spark of Radiance, which must be obtained from a variety of endgame content. Over time, you can utilize entire sets of crafted gear if you are willing to create them (with or without the help of the Crafting Orders system), but be warned that you cannot convert crafted gear into tier pieces at the Matrix Catalyst.

In addition to having customizable secondary stats, there are a variety of powerful optional effects that are either innately present on crafted gear or can be manually added with extra reagents. These bonuses are known as embellishments, and you may have up to two effects present on equipped crafted gear at a time.

5.1.

Ideal Gear to Craft

Crafting Disclaimer Due to an early raid boss dropping a Best-in-slot polearm that only a few specializations can equip, Brewmasters may opt to ignore the common first-choice craft of a weapon. If you will not be raiding The Voidspire on Mythic, then a crafted 285 weapon should be your first priority!

Due to the flexibility of crafted gear, such as the ability to specify your secondary stats along with your potential embellishment, you have many options available for gear to be created depending on items you have been able to obtain from other content sources. In general, however, some items are more likely to be useful for all Brewmaster Monks, which are listed below for convenience. For any items where you are able to customize their secondaries, we recommend using an Thalassian Missive of the Quickblade or Thalassian Missive of the Peerless to select either Critical Strike+Versatility or Critical Strike+Mastery.

Brewmaster Crafting Order (Optional) ( Blood Knight's Impetus) ➜ Loa Worshiper's Band ➜ Masterwork Sin'dorei Amulet ➜ Optional Crafts

Crafting Order Full Notes:

  • Weapons, such as Blood Knight's Impetus or Spellbreaker's Blade - Why? Made by Blacksmiths, crafted weapons can provide your biggest damage gain of the season. Depending on your goals for a character, crafting a weapon may even make sense as your first choice and can still feature a useful embellishment ( Darkmoon Sigil: Void). If crafted, you will eventually remove this effect by recrafting with a Lucky Keychain in favor of embellishing a different slot.
  • The Loa Worshiper's Band - Why? Made by Jewelcrafters, this ring comes pre-crafted with Critical Strike and Mastery, along with an unique embellishment effect that triggers roughly twice per minute and offers you a random Loa's empowerment based on your currently socketed gem colors:
    • Default (always available regardless of gems) — Blessing of the Capybara — 15-second Agility buff.
    • Garnet (Critical Strike) — Nalorakk's Call to War — For 16 seconds, gain a ramping Critical Strike buff.
    • Amethyst (Mastery) — Claws of Halazzi — Deal Physical damage to your primary target.
    • Lapis (Versatility) — Jan'alai's Flames — Deal Fire damage in an 8-yard AoE.
    • Peridot (Haste) — Akil'zon's Cry of Victory — 15-second Haste and Speed buff.
    All of these effects are helpful to a Brewmaster, though you are unlikely to socket any Peridot gems. The Lapis, meanwhile, tends to provide the largest damage gain, though you do not need to optimize your gems for these effects. Follow our regular gemming guide for Brewmaster instead.
  • A Masterwork Sin'dorei Amulet featuring a Stabilizing Gemstone Bandolier - Why? Made by Jewelcrafters, crafted jewelry is an easy way to acquire a large amount of your preferred secondary stats while also nearly matching the strength of the absolute best-in-slot choices available for the season. Prioritize crafting an Amulet due to the lack of useful choices available in Midnight Season 1. Crafted Jewelry also can access an embellishment that doubles the effects of the Loa Worshiper's Band: a Stabilizing Gemstone Bandolier. Consider this pair your best general set of embellishments for the season.
  • Embellished Armor - Why? Made through Leatherworking and Tailoring, any armor piece can be crafted with your preferred secondary stats. Although their item level will be slightly lower, many pieces can remain competitive with their best-in-slot alternatives and can even contain the Stabilizing Gemstone Bandolier embellishment instead of a Masterwork Sin'dorei Amulet if necessary.
  • The Hexwoven Strand (Optional) - Why? Crafted by Leatherworkers, the Hexwoven Strand is a belt that comes preset with Critical Strike and Versatility as its secondary stats along with a unique built-in embellishment effect. While worn, your attacks have a twice-per-minute chance to apply a 10-second Nature DoT effect to your target. Every time this DoT deals damage to the initial target, it will also attempt to spread to nearby enemies up to 5 times. While on the underwhelming side in single-target fighting, this item's damage potential rises in AoE, and can be a preferred craft for Mythic+ players in particular when considering its ideal secondaries. If doing so, you will need to recraft your Masterwork Sin'dorei Amulet with a Lucky Keychain to remove its embellishment effect.
  • Engineer-crafted Armor, such as Aetherlume Bands or Aetherlume Runners - Why? Although these items are made by Engineers, they can both be worn by players without the profession and uniquely feature only one secondary stat instead of two. They can also be embellished with some quality of life enhancements that do not count toward the normal cap of two, but can only be used in outdoor content. Consider making one of these pieces with either HU5H, Nonchalant Pup, Kinetic Ankle Primers, or M3DDY, Travel-Sized.
6.

Crest Usage

Midnight's first season includes currencies called Dawncrests, obtainable from practically all forms of content in the game. They can be used to upgrade gear up to Item Level 289, which is Mythic Raid Item Level. They can also upgrade Crafted Gear up to 285 as well.

Given the near-universal nature of gear upgrades, it can be difficult to figure out what items you should invest your limited amounts of Dawncrests in each week. As the decision will ultimately be based on what items you have been able to obtain, listed below are some guidelines and "wishlist" items to upgrade as soon as possible:
